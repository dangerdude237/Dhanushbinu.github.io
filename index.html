As a final year student at the Indian Institute of Information Technology Kottayam, I have developed a keen interest in big data and computer vision. This has culminated in the completion of two notable projects, both of which demonstrate my technical abilities and innovative thinking. One of my projects dealt with predicting future crime types in a specific area. I tackled the feature engineering challenge using an autoencoder to extract relevant information from crime location data and used XGBoost for classification. To handle large amounts of data, I implemented the solution on Spark. My project advisor was Dr. Vengadeswaran S. My other project was centered around detecting students who were cheating during exams. This project was supervised by Dr. Sivaiah Bellamkonda. I built a complex pipeline that extracted a student pose and converted it into a vector format. The vector format was then passed on to an Extra Tree Classifier model, which accurately classified whether a student was cheating or not. The project also incorporates various technologies, such as face identification and motion detection.

You can also find some of my previous projects below. If you haven't guessed already, my interests broadly lie within data science and big data.
